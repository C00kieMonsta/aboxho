# Writing About Me

> Use this when: writing a speaker bio, press mention, blog feature, or intro about me.

## Name

**Antoine Boxho** (pronounced "an-TWAN box-OH"). Use "Antoine" on second mention.

## One-liner

Software engineer and first hire at Scope AI, automating one of the most overlooked industries in the world with language models.

## Short bio (≤ 50 words)

Antoine Boxho is a software engineer based in London and the first hire at Scope AI, where he works across distributed systems, data pipelines, and fine-tuned LLMs. Previously he was in McKinsey's Core Tech & Analytics practice and a quant software engineer at FinMetrics.

## Medium bio (≤ 120 words)

Antoine Boxho is the first hire and a Member of Technical Staff at [Scope AI](https://scope.ai/) in London, where he builds production infrastructure, real-time data pipelines, and fine-tunes LLMs and layout models to automate document extraction in an industry that has historically resisted software.

Before Scope AI, Antoine spent four years as a digital consultant in McKinsey's Core Tech & Analytics practice, shipping ML-backed forecasting, clustering analytics, and automation for MedTech and Telco clients. He started his engineering career at [FinMetrics](https://www.finmetrics.com/) in Geneva, building calculation engines for portfolio valuation, VaR, and stress testing for large European treasuries. He also co-founded [Takeo.io](https://takeo.io/), a productivity startup.

Antoine holds an MEng in Financial Engineering from [Solvay Brussels School](https://www.solvay.edu/) (Cum Laude), with a year at the [National University of Singapore](https://nus.edu.sg/).

## Style notes

- Use "software engineer", not "developer" or "coder".
- "Scope AI" is the company name — avoid "Scope" on its own if context could be ambiguous.
- McKinsey affiliation can be described as "ex-McKinsey" or "former McKinsey (Core Tech & Analytics)". The Core Tech & Analytics practice is also known as QuantumBlack internally; only mention QuantumBlack if the audience will recognise it.
- Don't use superlatives I haven't earned. No "world-leading", "renowned", or "visionary".

## What to avoid

- Don't describe me as a founder of Scope AI. I'm an early employee (first hire), not a co-founder.
- Don't describe me purely as a "data scientist" or "ML engineer". I'm a generalist engineer who does ML when the problem calls for it.
- Don't overstate Takeo.io. It was a small productivity startup.
