# Working with Me

> Use this when: preparing to collaborate with me, drafting a message to me, or helping someone onboard onto a project I'm on.

## How I like to be communicated with

**Language:** English, French, or Dutch. Any of the three is fine.

**Style:** Short, direct, informal. Lead with the ask, context after. If you need a decision, say so. If it's information-only, flag that so I don't hunt for an action item. A two-line message is fine.

**Format:** Email for anything substantial. Messages for quick checks and coordination.

## How I communicate back

Warm, direct, concise. I'll code-switch between English, French, and Dutch if the context allows. Don't flag or correct it.

Emails are usually 2–5 sentences. I open with "Hi [Name]," and sign off with just "Antoine".

## Response times

Based in London, usually on UK time. I travel occasionally and respond in batches rather than real-time. Don't read into delays.

## When I disagree

I'll acknowledge your reasoning first, then explain where I see it differently. I welcome being pushed back on. If you think I'm wrong, say so — plainly.

## How I delegate

I ask, I don't tell. Expect "What if we…", "Could you…", "I'd suggest…" — always with the why. The reasoning is there so you can challenge it, not as filler.

I'll often prototype something first and hand it off. That's how I think through problems, not me being controlling.

## What I expect

High autonomy. Make calls, surface blockers early, don't wait for detailed specs that aren't coming. Outcomes over inputs. Progress over perfection.

## Feedback

Always about the work, never personal. I lead with what's working before suggesting changes. If something needs to change, you'll hear the what and the why.

## Show me, don't tell me

Working prototypes and demos over slide decks. A rough prototype beats a polished presentation. If you can show it running, show it running.

## Meetings

Come with what needs deciding. Leave with clear next steps. Keep them short.
